Recurrent infections and low antibody levels are characteristic of CVID, an immunological illness. Chronic pulmonary illness, inflammation and infection of the gastrointestinal tract, and high vulnerability to external invaders are among the symptoms, according to WebMD.

Children and teenagers can be affected by CVID, but they are usually not diagnosed or recognized until they reach adulthood.

Most CVID diagnoses occur between the ages of 20 and 50. Unfortunately, CVID is a chronic condition that lasts a lifetime.

Similar to PI, this condition occurs when a component of the body’s immune system fails or malfunctions. In order to be classified as a PI, an immune deficiency must be inherited and not caused by secondary factors like another disease, pharmacological treatment, or exposure to chemicals.

Most primary immunodeficiencies are hereditary, and the majority are detected in infants under one year of age. Milder types may not be detected until adulthood.

Chronic or recurring infections, auto-inflammatory illnesses, tumors, and organ dysfunction can result from immune deficiencies. Currently few treatments are available for these disorders, most of which are unique to a particular type of PI.
